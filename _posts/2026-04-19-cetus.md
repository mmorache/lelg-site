A few months ago I trapped a big tomcat who lived in the bushes behind my work. I named him **Cetus**.

He spent a week quarantined in my home, during whiched he sprayed everwhere, knocked pictures off the walls, and generally made it clear he did not appreciate the hospitality. After getting vaccinated and neutered, we went on a long drive to his new home in Seattle where a friend adopted him.

I've rescued several other office cats, but Cetus was a partifularly strange experience. He never liked me or trusted me and I can't blame him. I wanted to make his life better, but I don't know if he sees it that way.

I wrote a [song](https://soundcloud.com/loose-ends-long-goodbyes/cetus-demo-3-17-2026?si=811b046d26774069ae996399653ad577&utm_source=clipboard&utm_medium=text&utm_campaign=social_sharing) about it to help sort out my thoughts.
